# Extra Examples

These are intentionally kept in the repo because they are still useful, but
they are not the first thing a learner should read.

## Alternate / smaller examples

- `mcp-inmemory-notes`
  A lighter second example of the same-process in-memory transport pattern.
- `mcp-lowlevel-notes`
  A smaller raw `Server` example after `mcp-lowlevel-server`.
- `mcp-resources-prompts-notes`
  A smaller resources/prompts example after `mcp-resources-prompts`.
- `mcp-stateful-auth-notes`
  A combined example that teaches stateful HTTP plus auth together. Useful, but
  less clean conceptually than learning them separately first.

## Historical / compatibility examples

- `mcp-sse-weather`
  Worth knowing because SSE still appears in older MCP material and some legacy
  deployments, but not the first transport to learn in 2026.

## Educational approximations

- `mcp-dpop-demo`
  Good for the concept, not a standards-complete DPoP implementation.
- `mcp-multi-turn-sse`
  Good for the concept, not a definitive future transport spec.

## Why keep them?

They help learners compare:

- the full demo vs a smaller alternate example
- the cleaner concept-isolated demo vs a combined demo
- the preferred modern shape vs an older compatibility shape
